About
I'm currently an Applied Research Scientist and employee #7 at Ambient.ai. I work on building computer vision models that understand scenes from video in real-time.

Previously, I've worked as a Research Engineer at Google Brain, publishing research in large-scale distributed training and neural architecture search.

I completed my M.S. in Machine Learning at Carnegie Mellon University in 2017. Prior to that, I obtained my B.S. in Computer Science also at CMU in 2017.

My research interests are:
  • Solving multi-task problems in computer vision using compositional data primitives.
  • Using "outer-loop" optimization techniques like evolution to search for more accurate and efficient neural architectures.
  • Combining the fields of deep learning and high-performance computing to train larger and faster neural networks.
  • Applying machine learning techniques to solve the climate crisis.

Outside of work and academia, I'm an amateur photographer and a fan of anything outdoors (e.g. hiking, biking, camping). Fun fact: I really enjoy playing Dance Dance Revolution.
Research and Projects
  • NAS-Bench-101: Towards Reproducible Neural Architecture Search

    Chris Ying, Aaron Klein, Esteban Real, Eric Christiansen, Kevin Murphy, Frank Hutter
    NAS-Bench-101 is the first large-scale neural architecture search benchmark dataset, with over 423K unique architectures and 5M trained models. This dataset enables researchers to test their NAS algorithms in milliseconds by querying a pre-computed tabular dataset. ICML 2019
    [arXiv]   [poster]
  • Image Classification at Supercomputer Scale

    Chris Ying, Sameer Kumar, Dehao Chen, Tao Wang, Youlong Cheng
    Utilized distributed batch normalization, input pipeline optimizations, and 2D torus all-reduce to train ResNet-50 at petaFLOPS scale on a TPUv3 pod. We achieve 76.3% accuracy in 2.2 minutes, with throughput of over 1.05M images per second. NeurIPS 2018 Systems for ML
    [arXiv]
  • Depth-Adaptive Computational Policies for Efficient Visual Tracking

    Chris Ying, Katerina Fragkiadaki
    Proposed multi-depth fully convoltuional Siamese networks with parametric gating to perform efficient visual tracking. We train with a custom soft-budgeting joint loss but run inference with conditional computation to reduce runtime cost. EMMCVPR 2017
    [arXiv]   [poster]
  • Don't Decay the Learning Rate, Increase the Batch Size

    Samuel L. Smith, Pieter-Jan Kindermans, Chris Ying, Quoc V. Le
    Demonstrated both theoretically and practically that learning rate, momentum, and batch size are closely related and proposes an alternate strategy for training which enables larger batch training without accuracy loss. ICLR 2018
    [arXiv]
  • Large-Batch Training for LSTM and Beyond

    Yang You, Jonathan Hseu, Chris Ying, James Demmel, Kurt Keutzer, Cho-Jui Hsieh
    Developed a novel warmup scheme for training neural networks at extremely large batch sizes which enables speeding up training on large distributed systems without accuracy loss.
    [arXiv]
  • Enumerating Unique Computational Graphs via an Iterative Graph Invariant

    Chris Ying
    Proposed a novel iterative graph invariant for identifying unique computational graphs (colored directed acyclic graphs). This algorithm can be used for identifying computationally unique neural networks.
    [arXiv]
  • Improving Event Coreference using Knowledge Bases

    Chris Ying, Bishan Yang
    Utilize knowledge bases like YAGO and DBpedia to improve event coreference.
